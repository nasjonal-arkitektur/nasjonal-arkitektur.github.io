ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 7/WG 19: Techniques for Specifying IT Systems

About ISO SC7

SC7 delivers standards in the area of software and systems engineering that meet market and professional requirements. These standards convers the processes, supporting tools and supporting technologies for the engineering of software products and systems. Systems engineering, whose origin is traceable to industrial engineering, is defined as an interdisciplinary approach governing the total technical and managerial effort required to transform a set of customer needs, expectations, and constraints into a solution and to support that solution throughout its life. SC7, whose scope is Software and Systems Engineering, can thus be described as a horizontal committee who produce generic standards that are technology agnostics and independent of the application domain. These standards are principally focused on process models and good practices (Methods and techniques).

WG19 Mission

  • The development of standards to enable the integration of business and IT system specifications, and to facilitate the provision of software and system engineering tools and techniques to implement information systems.

  • Fundamental to this objective is the recognition that information systems must be realized in an environment where data and processing are distributed across heterogeneous IT resources and multiple organizational domains.

WG19 Terms of Reference

WG19 is responsible for standards for rigorously specifying information systems covering:

  • architectural frameworks for distributed processing systems, defining the structure of system specifications for both complete systems and for specific areas such as naming, security and conformance assessment;

  • metadata and representations of data for communication by both humans and machines, and the definition of the corresponding interfaces, such as data interchange formats;

  • modeling languages for expressing specifications of systems and their integration and distribution, and rules for relating different specifications;

  • functions to support distributed system operation;

WG19 ensures the evolution of the standards portfolio by:

  1. developing standards and technical reports;

  2. facilitating the processing of PAS and Fast-Track documents in its areas of work;

  3. providing the focal point for collaborative work with OMG and ITU-T on its areas of work, and with other organizations if required (e.g. IEEE).

WG19 Workplan for 2019-2020

  • WG19 Portfolio Management

    • The entire WG19 portfolio (50+) will be reviewed, resulting actions identified (confirm, retire, stabilize, transfer, revise), and required administrative items (resolutions, forms) prepared.

  • SC7 Modelling (aka architecture descriptions, specifications, …) Framework

    • WG04, WG42, WG19 and possibly other groups in SC7 (and elsewhere) develop artefacts related to modelling from different viewpoint.

    • A comprehensive high level framework will facilitate harmonizing concepts and terminology, and clarify scope of projects within SC7.

    • WG19 will work to prepare an initial version with the collaboration of other WGs. After review, It will be contributed to the SC7 Architecture SG as the architecture for one product line. Early publishing as a TR is to be considered.

  • Industry Modelling Framework

    • Although WG19 did produce early modelling frameworks (CDIF, ODP), new complex frameworks (TOGAF, UPDM, UAF,…) are done by industry group and consortia which have much more resources than SC7 and WG19.

    • WG19 will investigate the possibility of using the framework developed for SC7 to provide a mapping between these different framework if such an artefact is found to be useful to our client base.

  • User-Friendly Modelling Languages

    • Complex modelling languages have been standardized by ISO, other SDO and by industry and consortia. (UML, IDEF1X, ….). For the reasons stated above, it is not expected that WG19 could become a key player in this area.

    • However WG19 has in its portfolio simple modelling techniques that are widely used (flowchart, dataflow diagrams, decision tables,..) and supported by “office” level tools (e.g. Visio). Standardizing these, or refreshing existing standards would be useful for small scope modelling, done by small teams, individuals, or in an agile environment.

    • WG19 will investigate the possibility of refreshing current standards, adding some, to provide the market with a portfolio of standardized user-friendly modelling techniques.
